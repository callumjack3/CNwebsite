• 1.6 Describing access issues that need taking into account - describe issues that people with disabilities may face when using web pages and how we can overcome them.

People with certain disabilities can cover a wide range of factors. Over 1 billion people suffer with some sort of disability. If a website has some form of video media, it is important to provide subtitles and transcripts to provide for people with hearing predicament. Users with vision problems also may struggle if the website is crammed with images then they generally can’t be able to cope. Also with text, font, colour scheme and also its layout all contribute too. However, with text this can be averted with a screen reader tool so it’s important this is tested to read out the correct information. It may read the wrong information if text isn’t laid out correctly. Users can also find websites overwhelming if it doesn’t keep its structure. For example, one page being a completely different design to what they were previously on. This can be averted with keeping a maintaining consistency. The website should also have clear headings and buttons making sure they work all correctly for people who often suffer with navigation issues.

1.7 Describe which are the appropriate file types for websites.

Websites are often in .HTML format. This means I must name the file to the correct file format. Images are often used in my image too, in my scenario, it is .png’s and/or .jpeg’s. However, we can use other popular formats such as SVG and GIF. Jpeg is often the popular choice, the reason for it to be the most popular is due to its wide range of colours it can be saved on, over 16 million! Therefore, if often your images are have complex colours and shading JPEG won’t fail in delivering in it’s compression to your website. PNG’s also supports a wide range of colours, however it depends which one we are talking about as there are two different PNG’s formats. PNG-8 and PNG-24. PNG-8’s are capable of supporting upto 256 colours and PNG-24 has an unlimited amount. They are often suited for when your website has a lot of graphics and/or text. PNG’s often don’t compress too much, maintaining the original file quality. We can also use gif’s. Gif’s are good to use for banners, what makes them different to the other formats is that they can be animated as they are raster based. The quality isn’t as good, however they are useful for making little clips. They are often used on the internet for what is known to be called as “memes” but perhaps may be irrelevant in website design, in most cases. The last one, SVG is a vector format, therefore they’re not using any pixels, rather lines and shapes. As they’re not pixels, they are good for reshaping without losing any quality whatsoever.
